Yummy & Guilt-Free Dog Treats

Here’s a simple and healthy recipe for homemade dog treats that are perfect for your furry companion. These treats are low in calories and packed with nutrients!

Ingredients

    • 1 medium sweet potato
    • 1/2 cup finely grated carrots
    • 1/2 cup pureed pumpkin (not the spiced pie filling)
    • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce (make sure it has no added sugars)
    • 1 cup whole wheat flour (or oat flour for a gluten-free option)
    • 1/2 cup rolled oats
    • 1/4 cup unsalted peanut butter (make sure it doesn’t contain xylitol)
    • 1 egg

Instructions

    1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    2. Cook the Sweet Potato:
    • Wash and pierce the sweet potato with a fork.
    • Microwave it for about 5-8 minutes or until soft. Alternatively, bake it in the oven until tender.
    • Let it cool, then scoop out the flesh into a bowl and mash it.
    3. Prepare the Carrots and Pumpkin: If you’re using fresh carrots, finely grate them. If using canned pumpkin, ensure it’s pure pumpkin and not spiced filling.
    4. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potato, grated carrots, pumpkin puree, applesauce, peanut butter, and egg. Mix until well combined.
    5. Add Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the whole wheat flour and oats to the mixture, stirring until a dough forms.
    6. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/4 inch thick.
    7. Cut Shapes: Use cookie cutters to cut out shapes or cut into squares with a knife.
    8. Bake: Place the treats on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown and firm.
    9. Cool and Store: Let the treats cool completely before giving them to Mac A. Roni. Store them in an airtight container for up to a week, or freeze for longer storage.
